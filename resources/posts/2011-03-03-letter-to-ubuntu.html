Letter to Ubuntu 2011-03-03 18:00 Technology Rants

Dear Ubuntu / Canonical!

I have been slowly but steadily getting disappointed with your distribution. It started with using a color scheme that was just plain ugly, then it evolved to something even more gross. But I could live with that, because I could change it with a few clicks.

Then it went through moving the window controls to the other side, which was fine, as I wasn't using the default window borders anyway (I prefer my windows lean and clean, I don't need no minimize button, nor a close, I do want a sticky button, however).

Then you started to use and promote upstart, which I understand is godly and all, but it broke my update-rc.d: to disable a service, I couldn't do update-rc.d service disable anymore, I had to figure out how to disable services painlessly. I still prefer the old way, which worked for years.

But that's fine too, I could adapt, I could work it around.

Then you decided to design a unique font. I understand you want to go and promote your branding and all, but honestly, I can't see a difference between the new font, and whatever I was using before. But that's ok, it's your money, I just don't see the point, and changing the default font is a matter of a few clicks anyway. So I didn't mind this much, either, as I had a choice.

Then you recently decided to use Banshee as the default music player, which I applauded, as I'm a Banshee user myself.

But then you decided to castrate it, and make the useless Ubuntu Store (or whatever it is called) the default. Which I wouldn't mind, since I could easily switch back to something useful, which also happens to generate some money for the GNOME foundation. But then you made it so that the upstream default Amazon MP3 Store would make referral fees go to Canonical (at least most of it).

And this is where I gave up. Up until now, if I wanted to use the system as I please, I could, I had a choice. With this change, you took my choice away: if I want to use Banshee and it's Amazon MP3 Store plugin the way its developers intended, and give 100% of the referral fees to the GNOME Foundation, I will have to compile Banshee myself.

Granted, I can do that. But then I can use another distribution too.

Up until now, I have recommended Ubuntu to friends and family, but due to these issues and others, I will no longer do so, because even Debian is more friendly towards its users, even though it is perceived as mostly a geek distribution.

I will not thank you for all the years I've been using your distribution, because frankly, I didn't enjoy it all that much. My trusty old Debian gave me far fewer headaches, and didn't try to dictate what I want to do, it let me go and explore whatever and whenever I wanted to.

I still have one Ubuntu system running, due to company policy, but it will be migrated to Squeeze at the first opportunity (if so need be, I'll hook up an Ubuntu system in a virtual machine).

Yours truly, A Banshee user
